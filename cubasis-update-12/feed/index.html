Regarding getting your piano loud, the output of any instrument coming into a device such as the ipad gets converted from analog to digital , there is an ideal level you need to be at. Same with the output of any software to software or software out to a hardware recorder. The less extra stuff between sound source and recorder the better. I believe a compresser should help, my knowledge is little on this topic but i looked it up on gearsluts, hope this helps. There is a bunch of info provided here that should help you. And yes, cubasis is awesome, keep an eye on korg gadget though, its reallllly intuitive and the workflow of Gadget is the best for keeping it simple and just working well so you can focuss on composing. There is also the Bluetooth keyboard controller the Korg Nanokeystudio, its wirefree and gets about 5 hours on 2 rechargable AA batteries, reallllly nice for busses, trains and parks! the Gadget app was made together with the nanokeystudio so nothing beats it for simple. And output recordings sound real good as is. Both Cubasis and Gadget would be recomended purchases.
